10 facts about Sequoia National Park

Established date:
It was established on September 25, 1890
Location
It is located in the Sierra Nevada, a mountain range located between the Central Valley of California and the Great Basin.
The park is south of and contiguous with Kings Canyon National Park
Area/Size
The park spans 404,064 acres
Famous attractions
The park is famous for its giant sequoia trees and features the General Sherman tree , the largest tree on Earth.
Located in the center of the park is Moro Rock which is a granite dome
Animals that inhabit this park include coyote, badger, black bear, bighorn sheep, deer, fox, cougar, beaver, muskrat, and wolverine.
Visitiors can engage in various activities including hiking, camping, fishing, and backpacking
